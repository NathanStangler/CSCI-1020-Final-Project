Summary

Mission: Impossible is an action spy film franchise that began with the 1996 film of the same name. The franchise is based on the television series of the same name that ran from 1966 to 1973. The films follow the missions of the Impossible Missions Force (IMF), a secret organization led by Ethan Hunt (played by Tom Cruise) that carries out highly dangerous and covert operations to protect the world from various threats.

The franchise is known for its high-octane action sequences, elaborate stunts, and Cruise's performance as Ethan Hunt. The series has grossed more than $3 billion worldwide and has become one of the highest-grossing film franchise of all time.
